Impenetrable Design

The manufacturing process of the housing has a direct effect on insulators performance. Instead of assembling the insulator rod, weathersheds, housing and fittings with multiple interfaces, MSA implements the latest technique to reduce the interfaces and to make the product impenetrable.

Weathersheds and Housing

The and housing material is proprietary silicone rubber compound specifically designed for high voltage insulators. The proprietary formulation incorporates the inherent hydrophobicity and UV performance of silicone rubber while providing superior tracking performance.

Core Rod

The fiberglass core rod is the internal insulating part of a composite insulator. It is intended to carry the mechanical load. It consists mainly of glass fibers positioned in a resin matrix so as to achieve maximum mechanical strength

End-Fittings

The end-fittings are usually made by forged steel and are directly attached to the fiberglass core rods by a circumferential crimping process. This crimping process allows the end-fittings to utilize the rod's inherent mechanical strength.
